# 七咲式完成品チェック基準

Use this checklist when reviewing a completed website.

## 1. Blueprint Alignment

- Does the first view match the intended target visitor?
- Is the final CTA consistent with the blueprint?
- Are the promised sections present?
- Are the tone, design direction, and copy direction consistent?
- Are required assets, proof, pricing, access, or contact details missing?
- Did the implementation add unsupported content not present in the blueprint?

## 2. First View

- Can visitors understand who the site is for within 3 seconds?
- Does the headline name a concrete benefit or situation?
- Is the subcopy specific, not generic?
- Is the CTA visible without scrolling on desktop and early on mobile?
- Does the image support the service, atmosphere, or target visitor?
- Is text readable over images?

## 3. Conversion Flow

- Does the page move naturally from problem to solution to trust to action?
- Are objections answered before the CTA?
- Are CTA buttons repeated at natural decision points?
- Is button wording clear about what happens next?
- Is there a low-friction route such as LINE, reservation, inquiry, or consultation?
- Are forms, phone numbers, maps, and links working or clearly marked as samples?

## 4. Trust And Proof

- Are credentials, experience, access, process, FAQ, pricing, and profile included when relevant?
- Are testimonials and achievements real or explicitly marked as samples?
- Are trust signals placed near decision points?
- Does the site avoid suspiciously broad claims such as "地域No.1" without proof?
- Does the site explain what happens after inquiry or reservation?

## 5. Copy Quality

- Is the copy specific to the target visitor's pain and desired future?
- Does it avoid vague phrases such as "丁寧にサポート" without concrete detail?
- Are headings scannable on mobile?
- Are long paragraphs broken up?
- Does the wording sound natural in Japanese?
- Does the copy avoid overpromising, fearmongering, and fake urgency?

## 6. Design Quality

- Is the visual hierarchy clear?
- Are font sizes, line height, and spacing comfortable?
- Are section boundaries clear without making every section look like a card?
- Are colors consistent and appropriate for the industry?
- Do buttons look clickable and important?
- Are photos credible and relevant rather than stock-like or suspicious?
- Does the design feel specific to the business, not like a generic template?

## 7. Mobile Review

- Does the first view fit well on smartphone screens?
- Are headings not too large or too small?
- Are buttons large enough to tap?
- Are images cropped intentionally?
- Is there enough spacing between sections?
- Does text avoid awkward one-character or broken line wrapping?
- Are sticky headers, menus, and CTA buttons not blocking content?

## 8. Basic Technical And Publishing Check

- Are navigation links working?
- Are CTA links working?
- Are external links safe and intentional?
- Are image file sizes reasonable?
- Are alt texts present where practical?
- Is the page title appropriate?
- Is the favicon or logo consistent?
- Are sample placeholders removed before delivery?

## 9. Regulated Or Sensitive Fields

Flag wording such as:

- Absolute medical or health outcomes
- Guaranteed revenue, ranking, cure, weight loss, or legal results
- Before/after claims without context
- Fake customer voices
- Unsupported certifications
- Misleading scarcity or urgency

Suggest safer alternatives that describe support, process, scope, or user experience without guaranteeing outcomes.
